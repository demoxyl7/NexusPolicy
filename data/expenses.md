# Expense Policy

**Document Type:** Travel and Business Expense Policy
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Applies To:** All Employees Incurring Business Expenses
**Owner:** Finance / Accounts Payable
**Review Cycle:** Annual
**Classification:** Internal Use

---

## 1. Purpose and Guiding Principle

This Expense Policy governs the reimbursement of costs incurred by employees while conducting legitimate company business. The guiding principle is simple: the company will reimburse reasonable, necessary, and properly documented business expenses. "Reasonable" means an expense that a prudent person would incur when spending their own money under similar circumstances. When in doubt, ask before you spend.

All expense claims are subject to manager approval, Finance review, and periodic audit. Submitting a false, inflated, or unsupported expense claim constitutes fraud and will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, as well as potential legal referral for recovery.

---

## 2. Allowable vs. Non-Allowable Expenses

### 2.1 Allowable Expenses

Expenses are reimbursable when they are directly and necessarily incurred in the performance of company business. The following categories are generally allowable, subject to the limits and conditions described in this policy:

**Travel:** Airfare, train tickets, rental cars, taxis, rideshares, and mileage for personal vehicle use on company business (reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate, updated annually — $0.67/mile for 2025).

**Accommodation:** Hotel stays required for overnight business travel. The standard hotel rate cap is $250/night in standard cities and up to $350/night in high-cost cities (see Per Diem table). Employees should book standard room categories. Suite upgrades, resort fees, and minibar charges are non-allowable unless the standard room category is unavailable.

**Meals:** Business meals with a legitimate work purpose (client entertainment, team working meals, meals during travel). Subject to per diem limits by city.

**Business Communications:** Work-related calls, roaming charges during international business travel, and internet access fees during travel.

**Office Supplies:** Supplies purchased for a specific business purpose while traveling or working remotely (e.g., printing materials for a client presentation).

**Conference and Training Fees:** Registration fees for approved conferences, professional development courses, and certifications.

**Client Entertainment:** Meals, events, and activities for clients or prospects with a documented business purpose. Subject to per-person limits described in Section 7.

### 2.2 Non-Allowable Expenses

The following are not reimbursable under any circumstances, regardless of whether a manager approves them at the point of submission:

- Personal meals during non-travel days (lunch at the office, meals on regular commute days)
- Alcoholic beverages consumed without a business meal or entertainment context
- Traffic or parking fines and other violations
- Personal entertainment (movies, sporting events, concerts attended alone or with non-business guests)
- Spa treatments, gym fees, or personal grooming while traveling
- Clothing and luggage (except safety or protective equipment required for a specific job)
- Gifts to colleagues (birthday gifts, farewell collections — these are personal, not company expenses)
- Charitable donations
- Expenses for a spouse, domestic partner, or family member unless specifically pre-approved for a company event where their attendance was requested
- First-class or business-class airfare on domestic flights under 4 hours (see Section 3)
- Airline seat upgrade fees (unless necessitated by a medical condition with HR documentation)
- Travel insurance purchased independently (the company maintains a corporate travel insurance program)
- Expenses submitted more than 60 days after the date incurred (except where local law requires otherwise)

## 3. Flight Booking Policy

### 3.1 Economy Class — Standard Requirement

Economy class (coach) is the required booking tier for all domestic flights and international flights of fewer than six hours. Employees should book the most cost-effective economy fare available within a reasonable travel window (generally a booking made at least 14 days in advance).

Economy "basic" or "bare-fare" tickets that do not include a carry-on bag or seat selection are generally discouraged, as the add-on fees typically negate any initial savings. Book economy "standard" or "main cabin" fares through the company's approved travel booking platform.

### 3.2 Business Class — When Permitted

Business class airfare is authorized only under the following conditions:

- International flights of six hours or more in duration (e.g., transatlantic or transpacific routes)
- Flights where the employee is traveling directly to a major client presentation or critical meeting and must be rested and presentable upon arrival, with VP-level pre-approval
- Employees with a documented medical condition requiring additional space or accommodation, approved by HR

Business class bookings require pre-approval via the travel booking platform before ticketing. Retroactive approval for business class tickets is not accepted unless extraordinary circumstances prevented advance booking — and even then, approval is not guaranteed.

### 3.3 Premium Economy

Premium economy is an acceptable middle ground on long international flights when business class has not been pre-approved. It does not require special authorization beyond manager approval.

### 3.4 Booking Platform Requirement

All flights must be booked through the company's designated travel management platform. Employees who book directly through airline websites or third-party platforms without using the company platform forfeit their right to reimbursement unless IT or the travel management system was unavailable and prior manager approval for direct booking was obtained.

## 5. Expense Reporting Software Workflow

### 5.1 Approved Platform

All expenses must be submitted through the company's designated expense management platform (Concur/Expensify — see IT portal for current platform). Expenses submitted via email, paper forms, or spreadsheets will not be processed and will be returned to the employee.

### 5.2 Standard Submission Workflow

The standard expense reporting process follows these steps:

**Step 1 — Capture:** Receipt must be captured at the time of purchase using the expense platform's mobile app or uploaded within 24 hours of the expense. Delayed capture increases the risk of lost receipts and submission errors.

**Step 2 — Categorize:** Tag each expense with the correct category (Travel, Meals, Client Entertainment, Office Supplies, etc.), the appropriate cost center or project code, and a brief business purpose description (minimum 10 words, e.g., "Dinner with Acme Corp procurement team to discuss Q2 renewal terms").

**Step 3 — Report:** Group related expenses into a single expense report by trip or billing period. Submit the report within 30 days of the expense date, or within 10 days of returning from travel, whichever is sooner.

**Step 4 — Manager Approval:** Your direct manager receives the report for review and approval. Managers must approve or return reports within 5 business days. Reports pending approval for more than 10 business days will be escalated to Finance.

**Step 5 — Finance Review:** Finance conducts a compliance review for policy adherence, completeness, and receipt validation. Non-compliant line items are flagged and returned to the employee for correction or documentation.

**Step 6 — Reimbursement:** Approved expenses are reimbursed via direct deposit within two payroll cycles of Finance approval.

## 6. Receipt Digitization Requirements

### 6.1 Receipt Threshold

Receipts are required for all expenses of $25 or more. For expenses under $25 (e.g., a taxi tip or a coffee during travel), a receipt is strongly encouraged but not mandatory — a written note of the amount, date, vendor, and business purpose is acceptable.

### 6.2 Acceptable Receipt Formats

Receipts must be clear, legible digital images or PDFs showing: the vendor name, date of transaction, itemized list of goods or services, and the total amount paid. Credit card statements are not acceptable substitutes for itemized receipts. Screenshots of transaction notifications are not acceptable.

Receipts may be captured via the expense platform's mobile camera function, which automatically performs optical character recognition (OCR) to extract key data. Employees should review OCR-captured data for accuracy before submitting.

### 6.3 Lost Receipts

If a receipt is genuinely lost, the employee must submit a Missing Receipt Affidavit via the expense platform, attesting to the amount, date, vendor, and business purpose. Missing Receipt Affidavits may not be used for expenses over $100 more than twice in a 12-month period without triggering an additional audit review.

## 7. Client Entertainment Limits

Client entertainment — meals, events, and activities conducted for the purpose of building or maintaining client relationships — is reimbursable subject to the following per-person caps:

- **Business meals (lunch):** $75 per person
- **Business meals (dinner):** $150 per person
- **Client events (sporting events, shows, activities):** $200 per person
- **Annual per-client entertainment limit:** $500 per individual client contact per calendar year

All client entertainment must include documentation of: the client's name, company, and their relationship to the business purpose; the business objective of the entertainment (e.g., "contract renewal discussion," "new product introduction"); and the number of attendees (company and client-side). Entertainment involving alcohol is reimbursable only when it is incidental to a legitimate business meal — standalone bar tabs are not reimbursable.

Entertainment for prospective clients follows the same limits. Entertainment for former clients with no active business relationship requires Finance pre-approval.

## 8. Fraud Detection and Audit Procedures

### 8.1 Automated Controls

The expense platform employs automated rules to flag potentially non-compliant submissions, including: duplicate receipt submissions, amounts that exceed policy limits, expenses submitted outside the allowable window, expense categories inconsistent with the employee's role, and spending patterns that deviate significantly from the employee's historical baseline.

Flagged expenses are placed on hold pending manual Finance review. Employees are notified of flags and given the opportunity to provide additional documentation within 5 business days.

### 8.2 Periodic Audits

Finance conducts random audits of 10% of all submitted expense reports on a monthly basis. Employees whose reports are selected for audit are notified and must provide original receipts and supporting documentation upon request. All employees are subject to audit regardless of tenure, seniority, or historical compliance record.

### 8.3 Consequences of Fraud

Expense fraud — including falsifying receipts, inflating amounts, submitting personal expenses as business expenses, or colluding with vendors — is a serious violation of company policy and may constitute criminal fraud. Confirmed fraud will result in immediate termination, repayment of all fraudulently obtained funds, and referral to law enforcement where appropriate.
