# Onboarding Guide: Your First 90 Days

**Document Type:** Employee Onboarding Policy  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Applies To:** All New Full-Time and Part-Time Employees  
**Owner:** People Operations / Human Resources  
**Review Cycle:** Annual

## 1. Introduction and Purpose

Welcome to the organization. This Onboarding Guide is designed to support every new employee through a structured, meaningful first 90 days. Research consistently shows that employees who experience a strong onboarding process are significantly more likely to remain with an organization long-term, reach full productivity faster, and report higher levels of job satisfaction. Our 90-day onboarding framework is intentionally phased — each stage builds on the last, giving you the time and support you need to grow with confidence.

This document serves as the authoritative reference for all onboarding-related processes, responsibilities, and timelines. Whether you are an individual contributor, a team lead, or a senior hire, the same core pillars apply: compliance, culture, connection, and contribution. HR, your manager, your assigned Buddy, and the IT department all play coordinated roles in making your transition successful.

If at any point you have questions that this document does not address, your first point of contact should be your HR Business Partner (HRBP) or your direct manager.

## 2. Before Day One: Pre-Boarding

The onboarding experience begins before your first official day. Within 48 hours of accepting your offer, you will receive a welcome email from HR that includes your start date confirmation, a link to complete pre-boarding paperwork, and contact information for your hiring manager and onboarding coordinator.

### 2.1 Digital Pre-Boarding Checklist

You will be given access to our HR portal to complete the following before your first day:

- Emergency contact form
- Direct deposit authorization
- Benefits enrollment preferences (initial selection)
- Technology agreement and acceptable use policy acknowledgment
- Background check completion (if not already done during hiring)
- Signed offer letter (if not already submitted)

Completing these items in advance frees up your first day for more meaningful activities — meeting your team, understanding your role, and getting oriented to the environment rather than filling out paperwork.

## 3. Day One: Arrival and I-9 Verification

### 3.1 I-9 Employment Eligibility Verification

Federal law (the Immigration Reform and Control Act of 1986) requires that all employers verify the identity and work authorization of every person they hire. The Form I-9 must be completed within three business days of your first day of work.

On your first day, you will meet with an HR representative to complete Section 1 and Section 2 of Form I-9. You must bring original, unexpired documents to verify both your identity and your authorization to work in the United States. Acceptable documents fall into three categories:

**List A Documents** (establish both identity and work authorization — one document required):
- U.S. Passport or U.S. Passport Card
- Permanent Resident Card (Form I-551)
- Employment Authorization Document that contains a photograph (Form I-766)
- Foreign passport with a temporary I-551 stamp or notation
- Passport from the Federated States of Micronesia or the Republic of the Marshall Islands with Form I-94

**List B + List C Documents** (one from each list, used together):
- List B: Driver's license, state ID card, school ID with photo, voter registration card, or U.S. military card (establishes identity)
- List C: Social Security card (unrestricted), birth certificate, U.S. citizen ID card, or employment authorization document issued by DHS (establishes work authorization)

Photocopies of documents are not acceptable. HR will make copies of your documents for company records. The completed I-9 form will be stored securely in compliance with federal retention requirements (three years from date of hire, or one year after termination, whichever is later).

If you are a remote employee, a designated authorized representative in your area will complete the in-person verification on the company's behalf. Instructions for remote I-9 completion will be sent separately.

### 3.2 Day One Orientation Agenda

Your first day is primarily focused on orientation and settling in. A typical Day One schedule includes:

- **9:00 AM** — Welcome meeting with HR (I-9 completion, benefits overview, ID badge issuance)
- **10:30 AM** — IT setup session (laptop provisioning, system access, MFA enrollment)
- **12:00 PM** — Lunch with your manager and/or team
- **1:30 PM** — Department walkthrough and introductions
- **3:00 PM** — Introduction to your Buddy
- **4:00 PM** — Self-guided exploration time (review the company intranet, employee handbook, org chart)

## 4. Week One: Laptop Provisioning and System Access

### 4.1 Equipment Assignment

All employees receive a company-issued laptop on or before their first day. The standard equipment package includes:

- Laptop (MacBook or Windows device based on role requirements)
- Power adapter and relevant cables
- Headset for video conferencing
- Access to virtual desktop infrastructure (VDI), if applicable to your role

You will be required to sign an Equipment Agreement acknowledging receipt of the hardware and your responsibility for its care and return upon termination. Lost or damaged equipment due to negligence may result in cost recovery as outlined in the Equipment Policy.

### 4.2 IT Onboarding Session

The IT department will schedule a 60-to-90-minute provisioning session during your first two days. During this session, you will:

- Set up your company email account
- Enroll in Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) — mandatory for all accounts
- Install required software (collaboration tools, security clients, productivity suite)
- Connect to the company VPN
- Set a compliant initial password (see IT Security Policy for password requirements)
- Receive access to role-specific systems based on your job function

Access requests for additional tools must be submitted through the IT helpdesk portal and approved by your manager. Access is granted on a least-privilege basis — you will receive only what you need for your role, which can be expanded as your responsibilities grow.

## 5. Days 1–30: The "Learn" Phase

The first 30 days are about absorbing information, building relationships, and understanding how the organization works. Your goal during this phase is not to solve big problems but to listen, observe, and ask questions freely.

### 5.1 The Buddy Program

Every new employee is paired with a Buddy — a peer-level colleague (not your direct manager) who has been with the company for at least one year and has volunteered for this role. Your Buddy is your go-to person for informal questions about culture, workflow, team norms, and the unwritten rules of getting things done.

**Your Buddy's responsibilities include:**
- Meeting with you at least twice per week during your first 30 days
- Showing you the ropes during your first week (office layout, key contacts, communication channels)
- Answering "soft" questions you might feel awkward asking your manager
- Introducing you to colleagues across other teams
- Providing a safe space to share early impressions and concerns

**Your responsibilities as a new employee in the Buddy relationship:**
- Come prepared with questions to each Buddy check-in
- Be open and communicative about what is and isn't working
- Complete the Buddy Feedback Form at 30 and 60 days
- Respect your Buddy's time — keep meetings to scheduled durations unless both parties agree to extend

Buddies are not performance evaluators. Nothing shared with your Buddy is reported to your manager without your consent, except in cases of safety or compliance concerns.

### 5.2 Shadow Sessions

Shadow sessions are structured observation meetings scheduled during your first 30 days. They are designed to give you firsthand exposure to how different functions in the organization operate before you are expected to contribute independently.

**How Shadow Sessions Work:**

Your manager will schedule a minimum of four shadow sessions during your first month. At least two of these should be with teams or colleagues outside your immediate department. A shadow session typically lasts between 60 and 120 minutes and may include:

- Observing a client call or internal team meeting
- Sitting alongside a colleague as they complete their daily workflow
- Reviewing active projects, tickets, or deliverables with a team lead
- Attending a cross-functional strategy or planning session

You are encouraged to take notes during shadow sessions and debrief with your manager afterward. Shadow sessions are not passive — you are expected to engage respectfully, ask clarifying questions, and document at least three takeaways from each session.

**Shadow Session Log:** You will maintain a Shadow Session Log (provided in the new hire portal) that captures: the date, the function observed, the colleague shadowed, key observations, and at least one follow-up question or action item. This log is shared with your manager at your 30-day check-in.

## 6. Days 31–60: The "Connect" Phase

By the end of your first month, you should have a foundational understanding of the company's operations and your team's priorities. Days 31 through 60 are about building momentum — taking on initial responsibilities, deepening relationships, and identifying where you can add value.

### 6.1 30-Day Check-In

Your manager will conduct a structured 30-day check-in meeting. This is a two-way conversation, not a performance review. The agenda typically covers:

- Your manager's observations of your onboarding progress
- Your self-assessment against your first-month goals
- Any gaps in knowledge, access, or resources you've identified
- Adjustments to your onboarding plan if needed
- Initial performance expectations for the next 30 days

You are encouraged to bring your Shadow Session Log, a list of questions, and any early observations about process improvements or team dynamics. Candor at this stage helps everyone.

### 6.2 Initial Responsibilities

Starting in week five or six, you will begin taking on defined work tasks. These are intentionally scoped to be achievable — the goal is a successful first contribution, not an overwhelming workload. Your manager will identify two to three starter projects or tasks that are meaningful but low-risk, giving you the opportunity to demonstrate your skills and build confidence.

---

## 7. Days 61–90: The "Contribute" Phase

The final 30 days of your formal onboarding period are about full integration. You should be operating with increasing independence, contributing substantively to your team's goals, and building your professional network within the organization.

### 7.1 60-Day Check-In

A second structured check-in occurs around day 60. This meeting begins to shift the conversation toward performance expectations, goal-setting for the next quarter, and any development opportunities you want to pursue. Your manager will share qualitative feedback on your contributions so far and collaboratively set expectations for the remainder of the quarter.

### 7.2 90-Day Review

At the 90-day mark, you will complete a mutual review process:

- **Employee Self-Assessment:** A structured reflection on what you've learned, contributed, and where you want to grow
- **Manager Assessment:** Feedback on your performance, collaboration, and readiness to operate fully independently
- **Goal-Setting:** Formalized OKRs or performance goals for your first full quarter beyond onboarding

The 90-day review is documented and stored in your employee file. It is not a pass/fail evaluation — it is the beginning of an ongoing performance conversation.

### 7.3 Transitioning Out of the Buddy Program

The formal Buddy program concludes at 90 days, though many employees maintain their Buddy relationship informally. You will complete a final Buddy Feedback Form, which helps HR improve the program for future hires. If you found your Buddy relationship particularly valuable, you are encouraged to express interest in becoming a Buddy yourself after your one-year mark.

## 9. Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I don't receive my equipment by Day One?**  
Contact IT immediately. A loaner device can be arranged for the interim period.

**Q: Can I choose my own Buddy?**  
Buddy assignments are made by HR based on role, department, and availability. If there is a conflict or the pairing isn't working, contact your HRBP for reassignment.

**Q: What if my I-9 documents are pending (e.g., a replacement Social Security card)?**  
Notify HR immediately. In some cases, a receipt for a replacement document may be temporarily acceptable. HR will advise based on current federal guidelines.

**Q: Are shadow sessions mandatory?**  
Yes. A minimum of four shadow sessions during the first 30 days is required as part of the structured onboarding program.

**Q: What happens if I miss my 30-day check-in?**  
Reschedule within five business days. These check-ins are a required part of the onboarding process and should take priority over other meetings.
