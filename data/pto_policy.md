# PTO Policy: Paid Time Off and Leave

**Document Type:** Paid Time Off and Leave Policy
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Applies To:** All Regular Full-Time and Part-Time (≥ 20 hrs/week) Employees
**Owner:** People Operations / Human Resources
**Review Cycle:** Annual
**Classification:** Internal Use

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy governs all forms of paid time off (PTO) available to eligible employees, including vacation, personal days, mental health days, and paid leave for specific life circumstances. It also addresses unpaid leave and the conditions under which accrued PTO is paid out upon separation from the company.

The organization believes that time away from work is essential to sustained high performance, personal health, and long-term retention. PTO is not a reward for exceptional performance — it is a benefit available to all eligible employees and is encouraged to be used throughout the year rather than hoarded. Managers are expected to model healthy PTO usage and to plan team workloads in a way that enables employees to take time off without penalty.

This policy applies to regular full-time and part-time employees working 20 or more hours per week. Temporary employees, interns, and contractors are not eligible for PTO under this policy.

---

## 2. PTO Accrual Rates by Seniority

PTO accrues on a per-pay-period basis (bi-weekly). The accrual rate increases with years of continuous service, rewarding long-term commitment to the organization.

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Accrual | Bi-Weekly Accrual Rate |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 0 – 2 years | 15 days (120 hours) | 4.62 hours |
| 3 – 5 years | 18 days (144 hours) | 5.54 hours |
| 6 – 9 years | 22 days (176 hours) | 6.77 hours |
| 10+ years | 25 days (200 hours) | 7.69 hours |

**Part-time employees** (20–39 hours/week) accrue PTO on a prorated basis, calculated as (standard hours worked ÷ 40) × bi-weekly full-time accrual rate.

PTO begins accruing from the first day of employment. However, PTO may not be taken during the first 30 days of employment. After 30 days, accrued PTO is available for use with manager approval.

**Maximum Accrual Cap:** Employees may not accrue more than 1.5 times their annual PTO entitlement. Once the cap is reached, accrual pauses until the balance is reduced below the cap. Example: a five-year employee with a 22-day annual accrual may accumulate no more than 33 days at any one time.

## 3. Use-It-or-Lose-It Rules

### 3.1 Annual Carry-Over Limit

PTO does not carry over indefinitely. Employees may carry over a maximum of 10 accrued PTO days (80 hours) from one calendar year to the next. Any accrued PTO balance above 10 days that remains unused as of December 31 each year will be forfeited. Forfeited PTO is not compensated.

This rule exists to encourage employees to rest and recharge throughout the year rather than accumulating large balances they may never use. The company will provide reminders in October and November to help employees monitor their balances and plan accordingly.

### 3.2 Exceptions to Forfeiture

Forfeiture exceptions are granted on a case-by-case basis by HR and are limited to situations where an employee was genuinely unable to take PTO due to a documented business-critical project, a company-imposed travel or leave restriction, or a personal medical situation that prevented time off. Exception requests must be submitted to HR no later than December 15 and are not guaranteed. Granted exceptions allow carry-over for a maximum of one additional quarter (until March 31 of the following year), after which remaining balances are forfeited without further exception.

### 3.3 Manager Responsibility for PTO Planning

Managers are accountable for ensuring that team members have reasonable opportunities to use PTO throughout the year. Managers who consistently deny PTO requests without legitimate business justification, or whose team members regularly forfeit large PTO balances, will be flagged for review by HR. Creating an environment where employees feel they cannot take PTO is a management performance issue.

## 4. Process for Requesting Leave

### 4.1 Standard PTO Request (14-Day Notice)

For planned PTO of one day or more, employees must submit a leave request through the HR system at least **14 calendar days** in advance. This notice requirement allows managers adequate time to plan coverage, adjust workloads, and ensure no critical deliverables are left unaddressed during the employee's absence.

**Approval process:**
1. Submit the request in the HR portal, selecting dates and leave type
2. The system notifies your direct manager, who must approve or deny within 3 business days
3. A confirmation email is sent to you upon approval; calendar blocks are automatically created
4. For absences of 5+ consecutive days, HR is copied on the approval for workforce planning purposes

PTO requests are generally granted on a first-come, first-served basis. During peak business periods designated by leadership (e.g., end-of-quarter close, major product launches), PTO requests may be subject to blackout periods. Blackout periods will be communicated at least 30 days in advance.

### 4.2 Unplanned or Emergency PTO

For unexpected situations — illness, family emergency, or other circumstances preventing advance notice — employees should notify their manager as early as possible on the day of absence, ideally before their scheduled start time. Text or call if email is not accessible. Record the absence as PTO in the HR system upon return.

Excessive unplanned absences (more than 5 unplanned single-day absences in a rolling 12-month period) may result in a formal attendance discussion with HR, independent of the reason for each individual absence.

## 5. Mental Health Day Allowance

### 5.1 Dedicated Mental Health Days

In addition to standard PTO, all eligible employees receive **5 dedicated Mental Health Days per calendar year**, available beginning on the employee's 90th day of employment (prorated for employees hired after July 1 at a rate of one day per remaining quarter).

Mental Health Days are separate from and do not reduce the standard PTO balance. They may be used for any purpose related to psychological well-being, rest, emotional recovery, stress management, burnout prevention, or mental health care. No explanation, medical documentation, or manager justification is required.

### 5.2 How to Request a Mental Health Day

Simply request the day off through the HR portal, selecting "Mental Health Day" as the leave type. If you need the day on short notice and are unable to submit in advance, notify your manager directly and submit the request retroactively within the same week.

Mental Health Days do not carry over to the next calendar year. Unused Mental Health Days are forfeited on December 31 and are not paid out upon termination.

## 6. Jury Duty and Civic Leave

Employees summoned for jury duty are entitled to paid leave for the duration of their jury service, up to **15 business days per calendar year**. Employees must provide their manager and HR with a copy of the jury summons as soon as it is received.

During jury service, the company will continue to pay the employee's full base salary. Any jury duty stipend received from the court may be retained by the employee.

If a trial extends beyond 15 business days, the employee and HR will discuss options, which may include continuation under unpaid leave of absence provisions.

Employees are expected to return to work on any day (or half-day) when they are dismissed from jury service before 2:00 PM local time.

**Voting Leave:** Employees who cannot reasonably vote outside of work hours are entitled to up to 2 hours of paid leave on any federal, state, or local election day.

## 7. Military Leave

The company complies fully with the Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA). Employees called to active military duty are entitled to take the leave necessary to fulfill their service obligations and are guaranteed reemployment upon return, subject to the conditions of USERRA.

**Paid military leave:** The company supplements military pay to bring the employee to 100% of their base salary for up to **30 calendar days** per calendar year. Service beyond 30 days is treated as unpaid leave, though the employee may elect to use accrued PTO to bridge the gap.

Employees must provide their manager and HR with advance notice of military service as soon as practicable, along with a copy of their orders. Upon return from military leave, employees are reinstated to their previous role or a comparable role with equivalent seniority, pay, and benefits, consistent with USERRA.

Health insurance benefits may be continued during military leave for up to 24 months under USERRA, with the employee responsible for up to 102% of the plan premium during the absence.

## 8. Unpaid Leave of Absence

### 8.1 Eligibility and Approval

Employees with at least one year of continuous service may request an unpaid leave of absence for personal reasons not covered by other leave policies. Examples include extended travel, educational sabbaticals, personal projects, or family situations not covered by FMLA.

Unpaid leave requests require HR and VP-level approval and must be submitted at least 30 days in advance. The company is not obligated to grant unpaid personal leave and will evaluate requests based on business need, role coverage, and the employee's tenure and standing.

### 8.2 Duration and Conditions

Approved unpaid leaves typically range from 30 to 90 days. Leaves exceeding 90 days require Executive approval and are rare. During unpaid leave, the employee does not accrue PTO, and benefits continuation requires the employee to pay the full premium (employee + employer share). Employees on unpaid leave may not engage in outside employment that conflicts with their role.

### 8.3 Return from Leave

Employees must confirm their intent to return to work at least 10 business days before the end of their approved leave. The company will make reasonable efforts to return the employee to their original role; however, in cases where the role has been significantly restructured, an equivalent position may be offered.

## 9. PTO Payout Upon Resignation or Termination

### 9.1 Resignation

Employees who resign with proper notice (minimum 2 weeks for individual contributors, minimum 4 weeks for managers and above) and are in good standing will receive a payout of all accrued, unused PTO in their final paycheck. Employees who resign without proper notice may forfeit payout of accrued PTO, subject to applicable state law.

### 9.2 Involuntary Termination

Employees terminated without cause will receive a payout of all accrued, unused PTO in their final paycheck. Employees terminated for cause (gross misconduct, policy violations resulting in immediate termination) are not entitled to PTO payout, subject to applicable state law.

### 9.3 State Law Compliance

Some states treat accrued PTO as earned wages and require payout upon termination regardless of the circumstances or company policy. The company complies with all applicable state laws. Employees in California, Colorado, Illinois, Massachusetts, and other states with mandatory payout requirements will receive their full accrued PTO balance upon separation, consistent with those laws.

Mental Health Days are not accrued wages and are not paid out upon termination in any state.
