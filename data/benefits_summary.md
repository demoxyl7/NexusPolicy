## 1. Introduction: Understanding Your Total Compensation

Your compensation package extends well beyond your base salary. When evaluating your total compensation, consider the full value of employer-paid premiums, retirement contributions, paid time off, and supplemental programs described here.

This document is intended to be a plain-language summary of your benefits. It is not a legal plan document. In the event of any discrepancy between this summary and the official plan documents or insurance certificates, the official plan documents govern. Full plan documents are available on the HR portal.

Open Enrollment occurs each fall for coverage beginning January 1. Qualifying life event changes must be requested within 30 days of the event.

## 2. Health Insurance

The organization offers three tiers of medical coverage, each through our primary carrier network. All plans cover preventive care at 100% (no cost to you) when you use in-network providers, in compliance with the Affordable Care Act.

### 2.1 Plan Tiers Overview

**Tier 1 — High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP) with Health Savings Account (HSA)**

The HDHP features the lowest monthly premium of the three plans, paired with a Health Savings Account (HSA) that the company seeds annually.

- **Individual Deductible:** $1,600 / **Family Deductible:** $3,200
- **Out-of-Pocket Maximum:** $5,000 (individual) / $10,000 (family)
- **Coinsurance after deductible:** 80% covered by plan / 20% employee responsibility
- **Company HSA Seed Contribution:** $500 individual / $1,000 family, deposited in January
- **Your HSA Contribution Limit (2025):** $4,150 individual / $8,300 family (IRS limit inclusive of company contribution)
- **HSA funds roll over** year to year — they are yours to keep even if you leave the company

The HDHP/HSA combination is ideal for employees who are generally healthy, have predictable healthcare needs, and want to build a tax-advantaged medical savings account over time.

**Tier 2 — Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) — Standard**

The Standard PPO offers moderate premiums and lower out-of-pocket costs than the HDHP once the deductible is met.

- **Individual Deductible:** $800 / **Family Deductible:** $1,600
- **Out-of-Pocket Maximum:** $4,000 (individual) / $8,000 (family)
- **Coinsurance:** 80% plan / 20% employee (in-network)
- **Primary Care Visit Copay:** $25 (after deductible waived for primary care)
- **Specialist Visit Copay:** $50
- **Emergency Room Copay:** $250 (waived if admitted)
- **Prescription Drug:** $10 / $30 / $60 for Tier 1/2/3 drugs (30-day supply, in-network pharmacy)

**Tier 3 — Preferred Provider Organization (PPO) — Premium**

The Premium PPO provides the richest coverage with the lowest cost-sharing at the point of care, at the highest monthly premium.

- **Individual Deductible:** $300 / **Family Deductible:** $600
- **Out-of-Pocket Maximum:** $2,500 (individual) / $5,000 (family)
- **Coinsurance:** 90% plan / 10% employee (in-network)
- **Primary Care Visit Copay:** $15
- **Specialist Visit Copay:** $30
- **Emergency Room Copay:** $150 (waived if admitted)
- **Prescription Drug:** $5 / $20 / $45 for Tier 1/2/3 drugs

The Premium PPO is best suited for employees with chronic conditions, families with significant healthcare utilization, or those who value predictability in out-of-pocket costs.

### 2.2 Employee Premium Contributions

The company covers a substantial portion of monthly premiums. Employee premium contributions depend on the plan tier and coverage level (employee only, employee + spouse/domestic partner, employee + child(ren), family). Detailed contribution tables are published on the HR portal and updated annually. As a general reference, the company covers 80% of individual premiums and 70% of dependent premiums for the Standard PPO.

### 2.3 Dental and Vision Coverage

**Dental:** Two plan options — a DHMO (lower premium, in-network only) and a DPPO (higher premium, in- and out-of-network). Both plans cover preventive care (cleanings, x-rays) at 100% in-network. Basic restorative services are covered at 80%; major services (crowns, bridges) at 50% up to the annual maximum benefit of $2,000.

**Vision:** Annual eye exam at $10 copay; allowance of $150 toward frames or contact lenses; discounts on laser vision correction through the VSP network.

## 3. Retirement: 401(k) Plan with 4% Employer Match

### 3.1 Plan Overview

The organization sponsors a 401(k) defined contribution retirement plan administered through our plan recordkeeper. All benefits-eligible employees are automatically enrolled at a 3% contribution rate of pre-tax compensation on their 31st day of employment. You may change your contribution rate at any time through the recordkeeper's online portal.

**2025 IRS Contribution Limits:**
- Employee contribution limit: $23,500
- Catch-up contribution (age 50+): Additional $7,500 (total $31,000)

Contributions may be made on a pre-tax (traditional 401(k)) basis, reducing your taxable income in the year of contribution, or on an after-tax (Roth 401(k)) basis, with tax-free withdrawals in retirement. You may split contributions between traditional and Roth.

### 3.2 Employer Match: 4% Dollar-for-Dollar

The organization provides a dollar-for-dollar employer match of up to 4% of your eligible compensation. This means:

- If you contribute at least 4% of your salary, the company contributes an additional 4%
- If you contribute less than 4%, the company matches only what you contribute
- If you contribute 0%, the company contributes $0 — regardless of your automatic enrollment status

**Example:** If your annual base salary is $80,000 and you contribute 4% ($3,200), the company adds $3,200, giving you a total annual contribution of $6,400 — before any investment gains.

Contributing at least 4% is strongly encouraged. Anything less means you are leaving a portion of your total compensation on the table.

### 3.3 Vesting Schedule

Employee contributions are always 100% vested immediately — they are yours from day one. Employer match contributions vest on a graded schedule:

- Year 1: 0% vested
- Year 2: 25% vested
- Year 3: 50% vested
- Year 4: 75% vested
- Year 5: 100% vested

Employees who terminate before reaching full vesting will forfeit unvested employer contributions.

### 3.4 Investment Options

The plan offers a diversified menu of investment options including: target-date funds (the default investment), index funds across domestic and international equities, bond funds, a stable value fund, and a self-directed brokerage window for experienced investors. A licensed financial advisor is available quarterly for free consultations to help you build an appropriate investment strategy.

## 4. Mental Health Days

### 4.1 Dedicated Mental Health Leave

The organization provides five (5) dedicated Mental Health Days per calendar year, separate from and in addition to standard PTO. Mental Health Days are available to all full-time employees beginning on their 90th day of employment (prorated for employees hired after July 1).

Mental Health Days may be taken for any reason related to psychological well-being, emotional recovery, stress management, burnout prevention, or personal mental health care. No documentation, diagnosis, or explanation is required. You do not need to tell your manager the reason for taking a Mental Health Day — simply mark it as such in the time-tracking system.

Mental Health Days do not carry over to the next calendar year and are not paid out upon termination.

### 4.2 Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

All employees and members of their immediate household have access to the Employee Assistance Program at no cost. The EAP provides:

- Up to 8 free, confidential counseling sessions per issue per year with a licensed therapist
- 24/7 crisis support line
- Legal consultations (one per year)
- Financial counseling and debt management guidance
- Referrals to community resources and long-term therapists

EAP services are completely confidential. The company does not receive any identifying information about who uses the EAP or for what purpose. Access the EAP at eap.company.com or by calling the dedicated EAP line listed on your benefits card.

## 5. Tuition Reimbursement

### 5.1 Program Overview

The organization is committed to supporting your professional and academic development. The Tuition Reimbursement Program provides financial assistance for job-related education at accredited institutions.

**Annual Reimbursement Maximum:** $5,250 per calendar year (the IRS maximum for tax-free educational assistance)  
**Eligible Expenses:** Tuition, required course fees, and required textbooks (not including supplies, technology, transportation, or housing)

### 5.2 Eligibility Requirements

To be eligible for tuition reimbursement:

- You must have completed one year of continuous employment before your first reimbursement request
- You must be in good standing (not subject to a Performance Improvement Plan)
- The course or degree must be reasonably related to your current role or a career path within the organization
- Prior written approval from your manager and HR is required before enrolling

### 5.3 Reimbursement Process

1. Submit a Tuition Reimbursement Pre-Approval Form on the HR portal before the start of the semester
2. Upon HR approval, enroll in the course
3. After completing the course, submit your official transcript or grade report along with proof of payment
4. Minimum grade requirement: B (or its equivalent for pass/fail courses, a "Pass")
5. Reimbursement is processed within 2 pay cycles of submission

**Repayment Provision:** Employees who voluntarily resign or are terminated for cause within 12 months of receiving tuition reimbursement are required to repay 100% of the amount received. Employees who leave between 12 and 24 months must repay 50%.

## 6. Other Notable Benefits

**Paid Time Off (PTO):** 15 days annually (years 1–3), 20 days (years 4–7), 25 days (8+ years). Accrued bi-weekly.

**Paid Holidays:** 11 company-designated holidays plus 2 floating holidays for personal use.

**Parental Leave:** 12 weeks paid leave for primary caregivers; 4 weeks for secondary caregivers, for birth, adoption, or foster placement.

**Life Insurance:** Company-paid basic life insurance equal to 1x annual base salary; option to purchase supplemental life insurance up to 5x salary.

**Short-Term Disability (STD):** 60% of base salary for up to 12 weeks, with a 7-day elimination period.

**Long-Term Disability (LTD):** 60% of base salary after STD expires, up to age 65.

**Commuter Benefits:** Pre-tax payroll deductions for transit and parking expenses, up to IRS limits.

**Wellness Reimbursement:** $500 annual reimbursement for gym memberships, fitness classes, wearable devices, or other eligible wellness expenses.
